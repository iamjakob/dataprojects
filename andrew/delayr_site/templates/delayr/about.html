About Delayr

Delayr uses advanced machine learning techniques to predict domestic U.S. flight delays based on complex historical patterns. Parsing 10 years of raw on-time arrival data (>75 million flights!) from the Bureau of Transportation Statistics Delayr first builds a model containing the impacts on delays from a variety of sources (e.g. origin airport, destination airport, and carrier, just to name a few). Then whenever you give Delayr an itinerary (even for a brand-new route!) it runs your flight through its model to compute an accurate likelihood that your plane will be on-time, delayed, or cancelled.

Beyond the probability that your flight will be delayed, for a given itinerary Delayr will also predict how the time of day and day of week affect your probability of being delayed. Additionally, if Delayr detects that flights similar to the searched itinerary exist, it will predict their delay times as well and return the flights with the best on-time prediction. These features allow you to make intelligent, data-driven choices when booking air travel.
